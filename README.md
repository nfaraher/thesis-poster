## About the research

A three-study quantitative analysis of the role dehumanization plays
in turning political hostilities into motivated violent acts. The 
central finding is a suppressor mediation structure: partisan animosity 
alone carries essentially no violence signal (total β = −.050). The 
effect runs through **outgroup dehumanization** — hostility only 
predicts violence endorsement once opposing partisans are perceived 
as less than fully human (indirect β = +.085, 95% CI [.057, .111]).

**Study 1 — Meta-analysis.** Random-effects synthesis of k = 37 corrected
effect sizes across the animosity → dehumanization → violence pathway.

**Study 2 — Mediation analysis.** Bootstrapped mediation models
(N = 2,003; Landry et al., 2024 data) testing dehumanization as the
mechanism, including a party asymmetry: Republicans convert
dehumanization into violence endorsement at roughly twice the rate of
Democrats (Δβ = +.064, 95% CI [.013, .118]).

**Study 3 — Cross-national panel.** Fixed-effects regressions on V-DEM
data (118 democracies, 1950–2025) validating the polarization → violence
relationship at the macro level.
